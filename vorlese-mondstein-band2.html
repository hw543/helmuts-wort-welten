It was as if they were entering another world, a world full of magic and wonder. Yet they also knew this world was full of dangers, and they had to remain alert. Suddenly they heard a soft splashing nearby. Curious, they followed the sound and discovered a small, clear brook winding through the forest. The water glittered in the sun and invited them to take a break.

They sat down by the bank and dipped their tired feet into the cool water. It was a welcome refreshment after the long march. Finn pulled a few sandwiches from his backpack, and they ate together while enjoying the gentle gurgling of the water.

“Do you know what I wonder?” Arvid said thoughtfully. “How many fragments of the light stones exist? And where do they come from?” Leni and Finn looked at each other. They had asked themselves the same questions, but they had no answers.

“I don't know,” Leni said. “But I believe we’ll find out once we’ve found all the fragments. Perhaps there’s someone who can tell us more about the light stones.” Finn nodded. “Yes, and until then we simply have to keep looking for clues. The map will show us the way.”

After their break they continued their journey. The brook led them deeper into the forest, and they followed its course until they reached a small clearing. In the middle of the clearing stood an old, gnarled tree whose branches stretched like arms toward the sky. The tree seemed to be alive, and as they drew closer they noticed tiny lights glittering in its branches. They were fireflies, glowing in the dusk and lending the tree a magical aura.

“Look at that,” Leni whispered reverently. “That’s the most beautiful tree I’ve ever seen.” The three friends stopped and gazed at the tree in silent awe. Suddenly they heard a gentle voice that seemed to come from the tree. “Welcome, young adventurers,” said the voice. “I have been waiting for you for a long time.” The friends were surprised and a little uneasy. Who was speaking to them? “Who are you?” Finn asked cautiously.

“I am the guardian of the forest,” replied the voice. “My name is Eldrin, and I am the keeper of the ancient magic that lives in this forest. I have watched you since you entered the forest, and I know that you are looking for the fragments of the light stones.”

The three friends were astonished. They had not expected to meet a guardian of the forest. “Yes, that’s right,” Leni said. “We’re looking for the fragments to unveil the secret of the light stones and protect the world from a dark power.”

Eldrin seemed to nod, and the lights in the tree’s branches sparkled brighter. “You have a noble task ahead of you,” he said. “And I will help you as best I can. The light stones are powerful artefacts created long ago to protect the world from evil. They were broken into several fragments and hidden in different places so they would not fall into the wrong hands. Your task is to find all the fragments and reveal the secret.”

“Can you tell us more about the light stones?” Arvid asked, curious. Eldrin sighed softly and the leaves of the tree rustled gently. “The light stones were created by the old wizards who lived in this world long ago,” he began. “They were mighty magicians who kept the balance between light and darkness. But one day the darkness became too strong, and the wizards had to find a way to defeat it. They created the light stones, which contained the power of light and could drive away the darkness. But the darkness was cunning and tried to steal the light stones. To protect them, the wizards broke the light stones into several fragments and hid them in different places of the world. Since then the fragments have been waiting to be reunited to banish the darkness forever.”

The three friends listened intently. It was a fascinating story and they now understood better why their task was so important. “But why didn’t the wizards simply reunite the light stones themselves?” Finn asked. Eldrin sighed again. “The wizards sacrificed their own power to create the light stones and defeat the darkness,” he explained. “They knew they were not strong enough to drive away the darkness forever. That is why they created the light stones and hid the fragments, hoping that someday someone strong enough would come to reunite them. That someone is you.”

The three friends were deeply moved by Eldrin’s words. They now understood that they bore a great responsibility, but they were also proud to have been chosen to fulfil this task. “We will do our best,” Leni said determinedly. “We will find all the fragments and unveil the secret of the light stones.”

Eldrin smiled, and the lights in the branches of the tree sparkled brighter. “I know you will succeed,” he said. “And I will help you as best I can. Follow the stream and it will lead you to a safe place where you can spend the night. Tomorrow I will tell you more about the forest and its secrets.” The three friends thanked Eldrin and continued their journey.

They followed the stream until they reached a small clearing surrounded by tall trees. In the middle of the clearing stood a small, cozy house that looked as if it had come straight out of a fairy tale. The house was built of wood and had a thatched roof. A small fire burned in front of the door, and the smell of fresh bread filled the air.

A friendly older woman stood in front of the house and smiled warmly at them. “Welcome, young adventurers,” she said. “I have been waiting for you. My name is Elara and I am the keeper of this house. Come in and rest. You have travelled a long way.”

The three friends entered the house and were greeted by a warm, inviting atmosphere. The house was simple but cozy, with a large fireplace where a fire burned and a long wooden table laden with fresh bread, cheese and fruit. Elara showed them their sleeping places and said, “Rest and eat something. Tomorrow I will tell you more about the forest and its secrets.” The three friends were tired from their long journey and were grateful for the chance to rest and regain strength.

They ate together at the table and told Elara about their adventures and about Eldrin, the guardian of the forest. Elara listened intently and smiled. “You have already achieved a lot,” she said. “And I am sure you will fulfil your task. But remember that the forest is full of dangers, and you must remain vigilant.”

After dinner the three friends went to bed. They were tired but also full of anticipation for what the next day would bring. They knew there were many adventures ahead, but they were determined to accomplish their mission and reveal the secret of the light stones. With this thought in their hearts they finally fell asleep, ready for the next adventure of their journey.

The next morning they woke up early, feeling refreshed and full of energy. Elara had already prepared breakfast for them, and they ate together at the table. While they ate, Elara told them more about the forest and its secrets. “The forest you are in is a magical place,” she began. “It is full of ancient magic and secrets that have been preserved for centuries. The trees, the animals and even the stones have a voice and can speak to those who are willing to listen. But the forest is also full of dangers, and you must be careful.”

The three friends listened attentively. They knew Elara could give them valuable information that would help them on their journey. “What dangers are there in the forest?” Arvid asked curiously. Elara sighed softly. “There are many dangers in the forest,” she explained. “There are wild animals that might attack you, and there are magical beings that are not always friendly. But the greatest danger comes from the darkness that tries to steal the light stones. The darkness has many servants who live in the forest and are waiting to stop you.”

The three friends were a little unsettled by Elara’s words, but they knew they had to face the dangers to fulfil their task. “How can we protect ourselves from the dangers?” Leni asked. Elara smiled. “You already possess a powerful weapon,” she said. “Your friendship and your courage. As long as you stick together and help one another, you can overcome any danger. And remember that you are not alone. Nature and its inhabitants will help you when you need them.”

The bridge was old and rotten, and they hesitated for a moment before stepping onto it. But they knew they had no choice. Cautiously they stepped onto the bridge and crossed the river. Once on the other side, they breathed a sigh of relief. They had overcome another challenge and had come closer to their goal.

Curious, they followed the sound and discovered a small fairy sitting on a mushroom and looking at them with curious eyes. “This is another clue to the light stones,” said the fairy. “Touch the stone and it will show you the way.” The three friends stepped closer and carefully touched the stone.

Suddenly the symbols on the stone began to glow and a gentle golden light filled the clearing. The fairy smiled. “You are on the right track,” she said. “Follow the light and it will lead you to the fragments of the light stones.”

Chapter 1: A New Life at the Fire Department

Lukas stood there, uncertain, with a touch of nervousness, while flames flickered sharply in his inner eye as vivid memories. Since childhood, fire had exerted a strange fascination on him -- a powerful, all-consuming force that both attracted and intimidated him. Here, at the fire department, Lukas believed he had finally found a way to tame this tremendous energy and direct it in a controlled, meaningful direction.

When he first entered the station, he felt the cool concrete floor under his new boots and breathed in the characteristic smell of smoke, metal, and motor oil. The soundscape was busy yet calming; men and women concentrating on preparing for their next mission. His colleagues briefly looked him over, nodded to him, and went back to their work. No one knew his story, the emptiness that surrounded him, and the life he came from.

The first days passed quickly. Lukas learned how to operate the heavy equipment and completed exhausting exercises. Soon he discovered a new kind of fulfillment that the work gave him. But in quiet moments, Lukas felt the flame of his inner passion flare up again. The fire would not let him go; it drew him like a silent call under its spell. With each mission, the inexplicable, burning desire grew stronger to be very close to fire -- not just as a helper, but as someone who could actually control it.

One night he discovered smoldering embers at a small, abandoned hut on the outskirts of town. Lukas stopped, his gaze fixed on the glowing spot, and felt his heart begin to beat restlessly. He had an irresistible need to touch the last embers, to feel how close he could be to fire. A thought germinated in him, as shy and furtive as a flame hiding under a pile of ashes: What if he could ignite a fire himself?
